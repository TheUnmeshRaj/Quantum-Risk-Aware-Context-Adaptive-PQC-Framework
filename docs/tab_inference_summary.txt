1) Quantum Attack Sim - Key Inferences
============================================================
What this tab demonstrates:
- RSA-2048 is classically hard but becomes practically vulnerable under a fault-tolerant quantum model (Shor-style complexity).
- PQC families (Kyber/ML-KEM, Dilithium/ML-DSA, SPHINCS+) remain the recommended direction because they are lattice/hash based, not integer-factorization based.

Evidence from current simulation output:
- Estimated classical break time (RSA-2048): ~4.857690517597469e+12 years
- Estimated quantum break time (RSA-2048): ~4.77 hours
- Conceptual circuit size: 8 qubits, depth 14
- Gate counts: cp=10, h=8, measure=4, swap=2, x=1

Interpretation:
- The simulator provides a conceptual (pedagogical) quantum circuit and an extrapolated timeline model.
- Even with simplified circuit construction, the trend is clear: cryptosystems tied to factorization are high-risk in a post-quantum horizon.
- Strategic implication: migration should prioritize replacing RSA-dependent key exchange/signature paths with PQC-capable schemes.

Important caveat:
- The circuit shown is conceptual and not a full production-scale RSA-2048 Shor implementation.
- The break-time estimate depends on assumptions (logical clock, error-corrected qubits, idealized scaling), so use it as directionally strong evidence, not a precise operational SLA.

2) Migration Analysis - Key Inferences
============================================================
What this tab evaluates:
- Three strategies over a 10-step threat escalation with heterogeneous devices:
  1. status_quo  (RSA only)
  2. paranoid    (max PQC everywhere)
  3. adaptive    (device + threat aware assignment)
- Metrics: reward, total cost, breaches, crashes, compliance.

Current run results:
- status_quo:
  - reward: -5340
  - cost: 29,385,000.00
  - breaches: 54
  - crashes: 0
  - avg compliance: 0.0%

- paranoid:
  - reward: -700
  - cost: 2,346,666.67
  - breaches: 0
  - crashes: 20
  - avg compliance: 100.0%

- adaptive:
  - reward: -2032
  - cost: 9,676,333.33
  - breaches: 14
  - crashes: 18
  - avg compliance: 60.83%

Interpretation of tradeoffs:
- status_quo has the worst security and economics because breach penalties dominate over time.
- paranoid maximizes compliance and eliminates breaches, but causes substantial operational instability on constrained devices (crashes).
- adaptive balances constraints and threat progression, avoiding catastrophic breach counts of status_quo while reducing blanket over-provisioning relative to paranoid.

What can be drawn at program level:
- One-size-fits-all migration is suboptimal in heterogeneous fleets.
- A capability-aware strategy is required to avoid forcing heavy crypto on low-resource endpoints.
- Governance should combine:
  - risk scoring (QRI),
  - hardware feasibility checks,
  - staged compliance targets,
  - and device-specific algorithm mapping.

3) Combined Cross-Tab Conclusion
============================================================
- Quantum Attack Sim answers "Why migrate": RSA-era assumptions fail under realistic quantum threat trajectories.
